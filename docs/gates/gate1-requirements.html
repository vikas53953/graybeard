Graybeard — what we're building

An open-source Claude Code plugin that gives people like you — domain experts who don't code — the working discipline of a senior engineering team. It ships with proven laws, personalizes them to each user in a 10-minute interview, and (the heart of it) turns every painful session into a new tested law.

The three pieces

1 · Starter rulebookPROVEN ON YOU

The laws we built and tested this week — brief-before-code, neighbors re-tested, evidence before "done", fix-the-class, one question at a time, visual pages, the conductor pipeline with scoreboard and gates — stripped of your name, templated for anyone.

2 · Onboarding interview

One command. Seven questions or fewer, one at a time, each with a recommendation. Out comes: their personal standing orders, the every-turn reminder hook (installed safely), and laws with their name on them. What took us three days happens in ten minutes.

3 · The loop — the coreNOBODY ELSE HAS THIS

Something went painfully wrong? One command: point at the bad conversation → it finds the disease → proposes ONE general law (never a case-patch) → proves it with tests (fails without the law, passes with it) → installs it. Your Touch the Grass afternoon, productized.

Out of scope for v1 (on purpose)

Cursor/Codex ports · any cloud service or telemetry · team features · GUI beyond HTML pages · non-English onboarding. Small and excellent first; the community carries it sideways after open-sourcing.
